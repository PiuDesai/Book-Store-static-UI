THE RED TENT

"The Red Tent" is a novel by Anita Diamant, first published in 1997. The story is a fictionalized account of the biblical character Dinah, the daughter of Jacob and Leah, and is set in ancient Mesopotamia. It explores the lives of women in a patriarchal society, focusing on themes of sisterhood, motherhood, and spirituality. "The Red Tent" takes its title from the tent where women would gather during their menstrual periods and childbirth, creating a space of solidarity and support.
