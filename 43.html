GONE GIRL

"Gone Girl" is a psychological thriller novel written by Gillian Flynn. It revolves around the disappearance of Amy Dunne on her fifth wedding anniversary, and the subsequent media frenzy and police investigation that follows. The story alternates between the perspectives of Amy and her husband Nick, revealing their troubled marriage and the secrets they both hide. As the narrative unfolds, the reader is taken on a suspenseful journey filled with twists, turns, and unreliable narrators. "Gone Girl" explores themes of marriage, identity, manipulation, and the media's influence on public perception. It was widely acclaimed for its intricate plot, well-drawn characters, and unexpected twisits.

  • Author:Gillian Flynn.
