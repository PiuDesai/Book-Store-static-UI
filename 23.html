THE SCIENCE OF SCIENCE

The Science of Science" is a book by Matthew O. Jackson, which delves into the study of science itself. It explores how scientific discoveries are made, the dynamics of scientific collaboration, and the factors that contribute to the success or failure of scientific research. Jackson examines various theories and methodologies used to analyze scientific activity, such as network theory and bibliometrics. The book also discusses the role of institutions, funding, and incentives in shaping the scientific landscape. Overall, "The Science of Science" offers insights into the inner workings of the scientific process and the factors that drive innovation and progress in research.
